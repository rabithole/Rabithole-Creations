Photography Portfolio

    Portraits

    • My portraits have been about catching those moments that are fleeting in life. I pride myself in being able to have the camera settings right for those moments when the conditions are in perpetual flux. The only photo in this section that was planned is the last picture of the farmer girl Chyann. I really appreciated the tasteful way she pulled it off. It really was a Halloween costume.

    Landscapes

    • Landscapes abound. The outdoors is something I really enjoy. Like the portraits, they are about capturing those moments that we pass over so quickly. These moments happen everyday, but we are just to busy to stop and take them in. With the exception of the red desert scene, all these photos are from everyday places around Portland Oregon.

    Night Pics

    • Night shots are a lot of fun. They stretch your skills as a photographer and further your understanding of how bright a light is compared to total darkness. There is a delicate balance that needs to be achieved for the correct exposure. Just because the suns not up, doesn't mean there isn't much color to be had.

    Water Pics

    • Water is one of my favorite subjects to photograph. It provides many textures, reflections and transparencies you won't find any place else. Water can effectively double the amount of color in any seen creating very dramatic effects. Capturing the many textures that water creates is very challenging.

    Product Pics

    • The brownie pictures are for Oowee Gooey Brownies. They were my first venture into product photography, including Photoshop processing and manipulation. The goat picture is for a project I didn't get to Finnish. I found goats can be quite playful and had a lot of fun with this little guy. And last, the interior yurt picture is a composite of two photographs that I spliced together to show the entire interior of the yurts at Vista Park.

    Sunsets

    • Sunsets pose a unique challenge due to their often dramatic differences between darks and brights. I have turned to HDR to address this challenge. Coming from film, it is natural to feel as though using some digital techniques are somehow to easy or cheating. But then I realized the goal as a photographer has always been to recreate the moment as it was. Digital photography has made this goal possible in many ways. As with any tool, it can be abused. Although that is a matter of preference.

Web Projects

Vista Park Campground

Vista Park is an RV, tent and yurt campground located in the quaint little town of Skamokawa Washington. Operated by Port #2 of Wahkiakum County this project was a complete redesign. With great views and an amazing beach, I saw the need for extensive photography. Photographs were initially taken in RAW format then processed in Photoshop. Photoshop was used to process photos, create the Vista Park logo, park map, panoramas in the events page and the 360 panorama on the park photos page.

I created a park map using Photoshop to create an overlay over a Google Earth image. The maps layout is geographically accurate do to the method used in its creation. The printable map used by the office was given a few adjustments to aid the park staff in giving directions to visitors.

Much time was spent on the navigation as it is entirely done with CSS. I utilized code I had used in a class project. It was an extensive learning experience with nested lists and hidden elements. The right color combination was key as it needed to work on every page of the site.

I spent my summer 2014 term intensively applying SEO techniques to this site. Utilizing Google Webmaster tools and eventually integrating some Micro data using schema.org. I acquired tons of data and was able to increase traffic to the site with a combination of Facebook marketing and an effective landing page. The data showed site visitors predominately landed on the main page first and progressed to key parts of the site to find various camping information they might need for their stay. The scope of this project didn't allow me to explore other social media outlets. Something I know is important, depending on the project.

County Line Park

County Line Park is also owned by Port #2 of Wahkiakum County. A much smaller park that is favored by fisherman due to it's close access to the Columbia River. It did not require a site as extensive as Vista Park or a separate business image. I created a sub domain for this site, both to save money and as a learning exercise I utilized the templates I created for Vista Park to quickly create this site. By doing so I kept a common theme for Port #2 in an effort that visitors might make the connection that these two parks are part of the same operation.
